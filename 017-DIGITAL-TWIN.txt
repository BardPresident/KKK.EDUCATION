Consent is foundational to ethical digital twin development. Individuals must fully understand and explicitly agree to the creation, use, and ongoing maintenance of their digital twins. Data ownership represents another ethical frontier. Clearly defining who owns the data used to construct digital twins‚Äîthe individual, the technology provider, or another entity‚Äîis essential. Protecting digital twins from unauthorized access, manipulation, or misuse is essential. The integrity of digital twins also involves maintaining accurate, authentic representations free from distortion or bias. Transparent algorithms, regular audits, and independent oversight ensure that digital twins remain true and reliable representations of their human counterparts.
