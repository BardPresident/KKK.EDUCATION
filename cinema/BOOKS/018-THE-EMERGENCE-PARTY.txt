Universal Basic Income (UBI) has been widely discussed as a way to mitigate the economic impact of automation and job displacement. The idea is simple: give everyone a fixed amount of money per month to cover basic needs. However, UBI has several flaws that UBS corrects:

Inflation and Market Dependency ‚Äì UBI distributes money, but if the price of essential goods rises due to increased demand, its value diminishes. UBS removes this risk by guaranteeing direct access to resources rather than relying on market prices.

Wealth Hoarding vs. Direct Security ‚Äì UBI assumes that people will allocate their resources efficiently, but economic psychology shows that cash distributions often lead to debt accumulation rather than long-term stability. UBS ensures that security is not left to personal financial management but is structurally embedded into society.
